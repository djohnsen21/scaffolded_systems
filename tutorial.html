How to Build a Theory of Change Map

This short tutorial will walk you through how to create a Theory of Change map using real examples. Follow along and try it for yourself when you're ready.

Step 1: Start with the End Goal

Begin by clearly stating the long-term outcome you want to see. This is the goal that sits at the top of your map.

Low Income Residents of Medford Are Food Secure
Step 2: Ask "What needs to be true for this to happen?"

These are your preconditions. Add them underneath the end goal. They should be non-action-oriented — think of states of the world, not tasks.

Residents can purchase food
↑
Food preparation knowledge
↑
Food is grown
Step 3: Build out multiple pathways

Most outcomes depend on multiple things being true at once. Continue asking "what must be true for this" for each precondition you added.

Secure family structure
Transportation available
Knowledge about cooking
Step 4: Avoid action words

Instead of saying "host a workshop" or "distribute flyers", try framing your preconditions as facts: "residents are aware of nutrition" or "information is available".

Step 5: Try It Yourself

Once you understand how the map builds downward from an outcome using condition-based logic, you're ready to try your own.
